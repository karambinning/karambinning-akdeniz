The Story

A Turkish room on East Road, quietly excellent since 2013.

Akdeniz means Mediterranean in Turkish — and the name has been earned, plate by plate, on a stretch of East Road that Cambridge locals know well.

The room opened in 2013 as Merhaba and has become a much-loved Turkish address in the city — recommended at the British Kebab Awards in 2018 and still run with the same care a decade on.

The walls carry large atmospheric murals of Istanbul and the cities of the Mediterranean, painted by local Turkish artists. The charcoal grill works through the evening with the unmistakable sizzle of lamb shish and chicken beyti; the garden out the back opens onto Adam & Eve Street, cool under summer evenings.

None of it has changed much — and that's the point.
